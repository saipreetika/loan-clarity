# LoanClarity 💚
A free tool to help students understand, plan, and stress less about their education loans.

Why I Built This
I'm an Indian student who moved to the US for my graduate studies, funded by an education loan.
While I was studying, I didn't think much about it. Fees needed to be paid, the loan covered it, life went on. But as graduation approached and repayment started getting closer, I realised I had no real clarity on what I owed, when I had to start paying, what would happen if I missed a payment, or how the numbers actually worked.
I wasn't alone. Thousands of students across India take education loans every year to study abroad. Most of us understand the basics "I borrowed X, I pay it back in Y years" but nobody really explains the full picture: how interest capitalises during the moratorium, what a single missed payment actually costs, or how paying just a little extra each month can save you lakhs over the life of the loan.

LoanClarity is for every student who has ever felt anxious opening their loan documents. The goal isn't to scare you, it's to replace the anxiety of not knowing with the calm of having a plan.

What It Does
📊 Repayment Calculator
Enter your loan amount, interest rate, and how much you plan to pay each month. Instantly see how long repayment takes, how much total interest you'll pay, and how different EMI amounts compare, so you can set a real target instead of just paying the minimum.

Supports any currency (₹, $, £, €, and more)
Handles moratorium / grace periods — enter your own PMII amount and duration
Accounts for interest capitalisation during the moratorium
Shows a full comparison table across 5, 7, 10, 14, and 20 year scenarios

⚠️ What If I Miss a Payment?
Enter your EMI, how many months you might miss, and your bank's penal interest rate. See the exact financial cost, overdue amount, penal interest, bounce charges and a day-by-day breakdown of how missed payments escalate from a fee to a credit score hit to legal action.

💡 Survival Tips
Practical, bank-agnostic advice for navigating repayment, whether you're still in your grace period, just starting EMIs, or worried about a gap in income.

Who It's For
Indian students studying abroad on education loans
International students with loans from any bank or country
Anyone who wants to understand their loan beyond just "pay EMI every month"
Students in the moratorium phase who want to prepare before payments begin
